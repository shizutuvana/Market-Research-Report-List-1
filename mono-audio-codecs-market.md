<p><p>The mono audio codecs market is divided into two primary types: analog and digital. Analog codecs convert sound signals into continuous electrical signals, ideal for traditional audio equipment and applications requiring simplicity and minimal latency. In contrast, digital codecs transform audio into discrete digital signals, allowing for advanced processing, compression, and transmission over various platforms, enhancing quality and efficiency. Each market type serves distinct needs, with analog focusing on legacy systems and digital targeting modern, high-tech audio solutions.</p></p>
